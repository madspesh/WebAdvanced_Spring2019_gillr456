An accessibility assessment of coloured text on coloured backgrounds.

Is your web content visible to those with colour blindness and low vision? Find out the contrast rating out of 10 for various text/background colour combinations, and see whether they pass or fail the guidelines.

What is WCAG?

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 covers a wide range of recommendations for making web content more accessible to a broad range of people, including those with disabilities such as colour blindness and low vision.

Large text refers to regular text which is 18pt and larger, as well as bold text which is 14pt and larger. Normal text refers to regular text which is below 18pt, as well as bold text which is below 14pt.

An AA pass means that the colour combination has an acceptable amount of contrast (ie. above a 4.5 rating). An AAA pass means that the colour combination has an enhanced level of contrast (ie. above a 7.0 rating). Longer form articles that will be read for a significant period of time should have an AAA pass.
